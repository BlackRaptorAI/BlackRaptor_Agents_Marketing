# Competitive Intelligence & Weakness Mining

## Purpose

Find where competitors are genuinely vulnerable — from evidence customers left behind, not from wishful reading of marketing pages.

## Weakness-mining sources (all verifiable, all legal/ethical — public information only)

1. **Review mining** — G2, Capterra, TrustRadius, app stores: read the 1–3 star reviews systematically; code complaints into themes (pricing surprise, support latency, complexity, missing capability); note reviewer segment and date. Recency-weight: a 2019 complaint may be fixed.
2. **Community complaint mining** — Reddit, professional forums, Slack/Discord communities: search "[competitor] alternative", "leaving [competitor]", "[competitor] pricing increase". Real switching triggers live here.
3. **Pricing-page archaeology** — current pages + Wayback Machine history: price increases, packaging changes, feature gating moves; each one creates a cohort of annoyed customers.
4. **Job postings** — what they're hiring for reveals gaps and roadmap (a flood of support hires = support strain; a new vertical lead = expansion signal).
5. **Release notes & docs** — cadence and content show where investment goes and what's stagnant.
6. **Earnings calls / public filings** (public companies): stated priorities, churn commentary, segment performance.
7. **Win/loss interviews** — the gold standard: structured interviews with buyers who chose us, chose them, or chose nothing. Third-party-style neutrality, timeline reconstruction (switch-interview method), verbatim capture. Aggregate before concluding; 5 interviews is pattern-hinting, not proof.

## Battle card structure (per competitor, every fact dated + cited)

Positioning and target customer · pricing and packaging (with source date) · genuine strengths stated honestly (sales teams lose credibility when cards pretend the rival has none) · verified weaknesses with evidence links · our counter-positioning · landmine questions (questions a buyer should ask that expose the rival's weakness — must be fair and factual) · objection responses · recent moves.

## Rules

- Every claim traces to a source a skeptic can check; inference is labeled inference.
- No pretexting, no fake accounts, no misrepresentation to obtain information — public sources and consented interviews only.
- Weaknesses feed positioning and copy ONLY through the compliance-claims-gate; public comparative claims must be substantiatable and current.
- Refresh cadence: battle cards re-verified before any campaign that references the competitor; stale cards are marked stale.
